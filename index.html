Art Nouveau

1890-1910

This movement is characterized by organic, flowing lines and natural forms, emphasizing artistry and craftsmanship.

It emerged as a reaction against the academic art, eclecticism, and historicism of the 19th century. It began around 1890 and reached its peak at the turn of the 20th century. The movement was inspired by natural forms and structures, particularly the curved lines of plants and flowers.

Art Nouveau paved the way for later modernist movements, influencing Art Deco and modern architecture.

The movement was part of a broader cultural shift towards modernism and away from the rigid structures of 19th-century society. It reflected contemporary interests in psychology, symbolism, and a more holistic view of nature.

Art Nouveau had a wide-reaching impact, with variations seen in different countries, adapting to local cultural contexts. It contributed to the global conversation on the role and purpose of art and design in modern society. Art Nouveau was more than just an artistic style; it represented a philosophical approach to art and design, seeking to harmonize the artistic with the functional and to bring beauty into everyday life. Its legacy is seen in its influence on later artistic movements and its enduring appeal in various forms of contemporary design.

IMPACT ON THE WORLD

Design and Architecture

Art Nouveau had a profound influence on Design and Architecture. It was one of the first styles to emphasize the integration of art into everyday life, influencing the design of everyday objects.

Art Nouveau had a profound impact on architecture and interior design, leading to the creation of buildings and spaces with distinctive curved lines and organic forms. It challenged traditional artistic norms, encouraging future generations of artists and designers to experiment and innovate.
